# Team Dynamics & Leadership Skill

## Quick Start

Effective team leadership combines understanding group dynamics with strong people management.

### 1-on-1 Meeting Template

```markdown
## 1-on-1 Meeting - [Date]
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Frequency: Weekly recommended

### Agenda
1. How are you? (personal, check-in)
   - Work-life balance
   - Wellbeing
   - Personal updates

2. Progress on goals
   - What's working well?
   - Any blockers?
   - Support needed?

3. Development & growth
   - Skills developing?
   - Opportunities interested in?
   - Feedback for you

4. Team & company
   - Team dynamics feedback?
   - Company/org concerns?
   - Ideas or suggestions?

### Notes & Follow-ups
- Key points discussed
- Action items for next time
- Support to provide
```

### Effective Delegation Framework

```
1. Select the right person
   - Skills match
   - Growth opportunity
   - Capacity

2. Communicate clearly
   - Goal and outcome
   - Context and why
   - Constraints and deadlines
   - Authority level

3. Support appropriately
   - Available for questions
   - Check-in frequency
   - Escalation path
   - Resources provided

4. Follow up
   - Progress check-ins
   - Course correction
   - Celebration of success
   - Learning capture
```

## Core Concepts

### Leadership Styles
- Directive: Clear direction
- Supportive: Encouragement and help
- Participative: Input and collaboration
- Achievement: High standards
- Adaptive: Matching to situation

### Team Development Stages
1. **Forming** - Getting to know each other
2. **Storming** - Conflicts emerge
3. **Norming** - Standards and trust
4. **Performing** - High effectiveness
5. **Adjourning** - Transitions

### Psychological Safety
- Creating safe space to speak up
- Learning from failures
- Encouraging questions
- Diverse perspectives
- Respectful disagreement

### Mentoring Best Practices
- Regular meetings
- Goal-focused coaching
- Asking powerful questions
- Providing perspective
- Accountability and follow-up

## Common Challenges

**Challenge: Micromanagement**
→ Focus on outcomes, not tasks
→ Provide autonomy
→ Regular check-ins, not hovering

**Challenge: Hands-off approach**
→ Be available and accessible
→ Provide guidance when needed
→ Check-in regularly

**Challenge: Team conflicts**
→ Address quickly
→ Listen to all sides
→ Focus on interests, not positions
→ Work toward mutual solution

**Challenge: Low psychological safety**
→ Share your own vulnerabilities
→ Learn from failures
→ Don't shoot messengers
→ Celebrate questions and ideas
